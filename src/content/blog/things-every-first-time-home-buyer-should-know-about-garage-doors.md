# 5 Things Every First-Time Home Buyer Should Know About Garage Doors

Buying your first home is an exciting milestone — but it comes with a long list of things to learn. One area that often gets overlooked? Your garage door.

At Isabella Garage Door, we work with new homeowners all the time. Here are five things we think every first-time buyer should know about this essential (and often underappreciated) part of your home.

---

## 1. Your Garage Door Is the Largest Moving Object in Your Home

It’s easy to think of your garage door as just another exterior feature, but it’s actually one of the most complex systems on your property. It’s heavy, under constant tension, and opens and closes thousands of times a year.

Keeping it running safely requires regular attention — especially when it comes to springs, cables, and openers.

---

## 2. The Springs Won’t Last Forever

Most garage door springs are rated for about 10,000 cycles (a cycle is one open and one close). Depending on how often you use your door, that usually means a lifespan of 7–10 years.

When a spring breaks, your door can become inoperable — or worse, dangerous. If you just bought a home and don’t know the spring’s age, it’s worth having them inspected.

---

## 3. Noisy Door? It Might Be Time for Maintenance

If your door creaks, screeches, or rattles, it’s trying to tell you something. Many new homeowners assume garage door noise is just normal wear and tear — but it’s often a sign that your rollers are worn out, your tracks are misaligned, or your opener needs attention.

A little maintenance goes a long way and can extend the life of your entire system.

---

## 4. Safety Features Are Non-Negotiable

Your garage door opener should have functioning safety sensors that stop the door if something crosses the path. These features prevent injuries and protect pets, kids, and vehicles.

If the sensors aren’t aligned or functioning properly, it’s not just a hassle — it’s a safety risk.

---

## 5. Regular Maintenance Can Save You Hundreds

Like any mechanical system, garage doors benefit from regular inspections, lubrication, and adjustments. A simple maintenance visit can spot issues early and help you avoid the bigger (and more expensive) repairs down the road.
